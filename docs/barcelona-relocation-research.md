# Barcelona Relocation Research

## Scope And Disclaimers

This brief identifies planning inputs for **Barcelona Move Map**, a private localhost dashboard for a possible family move from Malden, Missouri to Barcelona around January 2027.

It is not legal, immigration, tax, medical, financial, or school-placement advice. Spain and Barcelona procedures change frequently; the dashboard should track sources, dates checked, confidence, and professional-advice flags.

## Confirmed Facts From Sources

- Spain offers a telework/digital nomad visa for non-EU foreigners doing remote work or professional activity for companies outside Spain, with family-member paths listed for spouse/partner and dependent children. The Washington consulate says US passport holders can also be legally in Spain visa-free for 90 days in a 180-day period, and some foreigners legally in Spain can apply directly in Spain for a telework residence permit. Source: Spanish Consulate in Washington, Telework Visa.
- Spain offers a non-working/non-lucrative residence visa for people who can prove sufficient means and do not carry out gainful work or professional activity in Spain. The Los Angeles consulate page states this visa does not allow teleworking. Source: Spanish Consulate in Los Angeles, Non-working Residence Visa.
- Barcelona preschool education covers ages 0 to 6 and is not compulsory. The first cycle, ages 0 to 3, includes municipal nursery schools and day care centres; the second cycle, ages 3 to 6, usually takes place in primary schools. Public second-cycle preschool is described as free and universal, with possible charges for meals, trips, or supplies. Source: Barcelona International Welcome, Pre-school Education.
- Barcelona municipal register registration, or padrón/empadronamiento, is compulsory for everyone living in Barcelona and gives access to rights such as public healthcare and basic schooling for children. Source: Barcelona International Welcome, Municipal Register.
- Each family member needs an individual healthcare card, TSI, to access Catalan public healthcare services; the TSI procedure may depend on municipal registration, recognition of healthcare rights, Social Security status, beneficiary status, or second-level CatSalut access. Source: Barcelona International Welcome, TSI.
- Barcelona has 10 districts and 73 neighbourhoods visible through the official city neighbourhood map. Source: Barcelona City Council, Living in the Neighbourhood.

## Assumptions To Track Separately

- Nate's household will need a long-stay residence path if staying beyond Schengen short-stay limits.
- Because the family includes a young child, school catchment, language environment, healthcare access, commute, green space, and housing stability should be tracked as first-class comparison factors.
- The first prototype should use planning categories and sourced placeholders rather than definitive recommendations.
- Budget amounts should start as ranges with confidence levels until the family researches current rents, insurance, school costs, visa costs, and income/tax implications.

## 1. Visa / Residency Planning Topics

Track visa and residency paths as options, not decisions. Each path should include requirements, family eligibility, work restrictions, timing, costs, evidence required, source links, and professional-advice flags.

Potential paths to compare:
- **Telework / digital nomad visa or residence authorization**: remote work for non-Spanish companies; family-member rules; income thresholds; employment vs self-employment requirements; Social Security questions; NIE requirement; consular jurisdiction; whether applying from the US or in Spain is practical.
- **Non-lucrative residence visa**: residence without work; financial means; private/public health insurance requirements; family documents; restriction that the Los Angeles consulate says this visa does not allow teleworking.
- **Student or education-related visas**: relevant only if a parent plans qualifying study; not assumed.
- **Work authorization / employee visa**: relevant only if Spanish employment or sponsorship becomes realistic; not assumed.
- **Short-stay exploratory travel**: 90/180 Schengen stay tracking for research trips, school visits, housing scouting, and appointments; not a relocation solution by itself.

Dashboard questions:
- Which consulate has jurisdiction for a Missouri resident at the time of application?
- Which paths allow the family's intended income/work model?
- What income/assets are required for Nate, spouse, and child?
- What documents expire or must be issued within a recent window?
- Which documents need apostille, certified translation, originals, and copies?
- What is the realistic application timeline before January 2027?
- What requires an immigration attorney or qualified tax advisor?

Professional advice flags:
- Immigration eligibility and application strategy.
- Whether US remote work, self-employment, or business income fits a specific Spanish residence path.
- Spanish tax residency and US tax interaction.
- Social Security obligations for remote work.

## 3. Childcare / Schooling Questions For A Three-Year-Old

Confirmed from Barcelona International Welcome:
- Preschool is ages 0 to 6 and not compulsory.
- First cycle is 0 to 3; second cycle is 3 to 6.
- Public second-cycle preschool is described as free and universal, with additional charges possible for meals, trips, or supplies.
- Public 0 to 3 places are not universal and may be allocated through an annual process.
- Barcelona has municipal child-rearing spaces for children up to age three and families.

Dashboard questions:
- What age will the child be in September 2026 and September 2027?
- Which cycle applies on arrival around January 2027?
- What school year timing matters if arriving mid-year?
- What documents are required for enrollment?
- Does enrollment require padrón registration at the Barcelona address?
- Which schools serve each candidate neighborhood?
- What language environment is best: Catalan, Spanish, English, bilingual, international, Montessori, etc.?
- Public, concertada, private, or international school?
- What are application windows, open days, waitlists, fees, meals, supplies, and after-school options?
- How will a mid-year arrival affect social transition and family stability?

Seed categories:
- Public preschool, private preschool, international school, municipal nursery, child-rearing spaces, language support, after-school care.

Professional advice flags:
- School enrollment requirements for a foreign child.
- Special education, healthcare, vaccination, or developmental support needs if applicable.

## 4. Healthcare / Insurance Planning Topics

Confirmed from Barcelona International Welcome:
- TSI is individual and each family member needs their own card.
- TSI is used to access a family doctor, appointments, health centres, public hospitals, and subsidised medications.
- Municipal registration and recognition of healthcare rights may be required depending on status.
- Foreigners without insured or beneficiary status may have separate CatSalut access procedures.

Dashboard questions:
- What insurance is required for the selected visa path?
- Must private insurance be no deductible/no copay/no waiting period/no coverage limit for visa purposes?
- Which insurers are authorized to operate in Spain?
- When can each family member access CatSalut or TSI?
- What steps happen before and after arrival: visa insurance, padrón, healthcare rights recognition, TSI, family doctor assignment?
- What pediatric care options are nearby in each candidate neighborhood?
- What prescriptions, medical records, vaccination records, and translations are needed before travel?
- What coverage is needed during transition travel and first months?

Professional advice flags:
- Medical insurance compliance for visa applications.
- Public healthcare eligibility for the selected residence status.
- Medical advice, continuity of care, prescriptions, vaccinations, and pediatric needs.

- The city provides an official district/neighbourhood map.

- Proximity to schools/preschools and application catchment areas.

- Commute needs and transit connections.
- Noise, walkability, air quality, perceived safety, building accessibility, elevator, stroller practicality.
- Language/community fit and international-family support.
- Temporary lodging vs long-term rental plan.
- Arrival-month availability around January.

Dashboard fields:
- Neighborhood/district, housing type, rent range, pros, cons, family fit, transit fit, school fit, healthcare fit, confidence, source links, visit notes, related risks.

Professional advice flags:
- Lease review and tenant rights.
- Whether a housing arrangement supports padrón registration.
- Tax/residency implications of housing timing.
